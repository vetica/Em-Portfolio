I'm Em Poertner —
designer, writer and editor
from Roanoke, Virginia.

I’ve always struggled to write ‘about’ sections for myself because I fluctuate between being a closed book and an oversharer. Plus I’m never quite sure what people actually want to glean from these things.

I live and work in Roanoke, Virginia - a place I picked because of childhood ties and because it’s absolutely beautiful 365 days a year (also there’s a ton of great craft beer.)

I started my career working for the Louisville Design Center. In my time there, I designed print newspaper sections for sites as small as the Fremont (Ohio) News-Messenger to as large as the Detroit Free Press. It was in Louisville that I ran my favorite 5K - the Hungry Turkey Run - a Thanksgiving 5K where at the end runners were given giant cinnamon rolls (I believe they now give out giant donuts, which is equally as amazing.)

In 2022, I started working for the USA Today Network - Tennessee as a sports planning editor and project specialist. I did a little bit of everything including, but not limited to, managing project timelines, planning sports sections, writing newsletters and designing visuals.

Since June of 2024, I’ve worked for Roanoke County as a Public Information Specialist. I’ve been told that sounds boring, but most of my friends (much to my chagrin) call me an influencer. I create videos for YouTube and social media including writing, planning, hosting and, for some short-form content, videography and editing. Not every day is sexy, some days I spend the better half of an hour debating how long a newsletter introduction should be.

In my non-work life, I play Ultimate, run, hike, read, write, draw, garden, craft, do DIYs, and watch sports. I am a Packers fan and I do think drafting a wide receiver in the first round of the draft you’re hosting (when you haven’t drafted one when you needed to) means there is a script.

Resume
Roanoke County, Cave Spring, VA — Public Information Specialist
JUNE 2024 - PRESENT

When I got this job, my friends started calling me an influencer, which is a little cringe. But I do write, plan and host a monthly podcast and two video series that promote Roanoke County. I also collaborate with departments to create short-form content for social media, including writing, planning, videography and editing.

I also develop promotional material for the county as necessary including articles for the website and printed material for events. Plus, I write for and edit the weekly internal employee newsletter.

USA Today Network - Tennessee, Remote — Sports Planning Editor & Project Specialist
JAN 2022 - JUNE 2024

At the core of my job, I collaborated with editors, reporters and the production team to build content plans for USA Today Network - Tennessee publications including the Nashville Tennessean and the Knoxville News Sentinel. I used audience analytics from Pars.ly and Google Analytics to inform our digital strategy and grow subscriptions. I also curated, edited and helped write newsletters for different target audiences including Nashville’s Daily Briefing.

For our enterprise work, I managed the project’s timeline and developed compelling visuals for on-platform and off-platform promotion.

USA Today Network Design Center, Remote — Team Leader
APRIL 2021 - JAN 2022

As a team leader, I assisted in managing the production of daily print sports sections for the USA Today Network's Midwest and Ohio regions. I mentored designers and assisted with training, scheduling staff and employee relations. I also designed and page edited daily print sports sections and special projects.

During my time with the studio, including my time as a designer, I was part of the Diversity Steering Committee. As part of the group, I helped influence department policies around how we represent diverse communities in the newspaper. I designed, edited and helped write the intradepartmental newsletter our team created.

Louisville Design Studio/USA Today Network Design Center, Louisville, KY — Designer & Page Editor
SEPT 2016 - APRIL 2021

I was hired by the Louisville Design Studio after graduating from Roanoke College in 2016. I designed and page edited daily news and sports sections for USA Today Network publications including the Louisville Courier Journal and Cincinnati Enquirer. Football season was always our busiest time. I worked on a variety of special sections including the 2020 Alabama Gameday wraps (for the Tuscaloosa News), the 2019 Clemson Gameday section (for the Greenville News) and high school football preview sections galore.

Creasey Mahan Nature Preserve, Goshen, KY — Freelancer
MARCH 2019 - APRIL 2022

I started volunteering at Creasey Mahan in its woodland gardens in 2018 and was brought on as a freelancer in 2019 to help with the Butterfly Ball, an annual fundraising gala. I designed the program for the gala and many other posters and printed material for the events at the preserve from 2019-2022.

In 2020, a lot of my efforts for Creasey Mahan switched to social media promotion. I created posts that highlighted the trails and wildlife at the preserve since in-person educational events were cancelled.
